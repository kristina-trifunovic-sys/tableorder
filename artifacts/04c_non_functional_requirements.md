# non_functional_requirements

## quality_areas

| quality_area_id | quality_area_name | area_description | related_br_ids |
|---|---|---|---|
| QA-001 | Performance | Response time and throughput requirements that ensure the system is fast enough for real-time in-venue use. | [BR-001](01_business_requirements.md#br-001); [BR-002](01_business_requirements.md#br-002) |
| QA-002 | Availability & Reliability | Requirements ensuring the system remains operational throughout venue service hours and that confirmed orders are not lost. | [BR-001](01_business_requirements.md#br-001); [BR-002](01_business_requirements.md#br-002); [BR-003](01_business_requirements.md#br-003) |
| QA-003 | Security | Requirements governing the protection of payment data, customer personal data, and administrative access. | [BR-002](01_business_requirements.md#br-002); [BR-004](01_business_requirements.md#br-004) |
| QA-004 | Usability | Requirements ensuring the customer-facing app is operable by first-time users of mixed ages without external assistance, on personal mobile devices. | [BR-001](01_business_requirements.md#br-001); [BR-004](01_business_requirements.md#br-004) |
| QA-005 | Scalability | Requirements ensuring the system can handle the maximum expected concurrent load at the venue. | [BR-001](01_business_requirements.md#br-001); [BR-002](01_business_requirements.md#br-002) |
| QA-006 | Maintainability & Operability | Requirements ensuring the system is diagnosable and patchable by the on-standby technical team with minimal service disruption. | [BR-001](01_business_requirements.md#br-001); [BR-002](01_business_requirements.md#br-002) |

## requirement_catalog

| nfr_id | quality_area_id | requirement_name | target_or_condition | applies_when | rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| NFR-001 | [QA-001](#qa-001) | Order & Payment Confirmation Speed | The order confirmation screen must appear within 3 seconds of the customer submitting their order | During self-service order submission, for both card and cash payment methods | Confirmation is the most critical moment in the customer journey; delays at checkout create friction and undermine trust, especially for card payments involving a payment gateway. |
| NFR-002 | [QA-001](#qa-001) | Menu Load Time | The menu must be fully loaded and browsable within 3 seconds of the customer first accessing it | When a customer accesses the menu after session initiation via QR code scan | A slow menu load is the first impression of the ordering experience; delays here increase abandonment and erode the efficiency gains the system is designed to deliver. |
| NFR-003 | [QA-002](#qa-002) | Service Availability | The system must be operational throughout all venue service hours; brief unplanned outages of a few minutes are acceptable | During all venue operating hours | The system must support continuous service delivery during the venue's active trading period; extended outages would force a return to fully manual service. |
| NFR-004 | [QA-002](#qa-002) | Order Durability | A confirmed order must not be lost or corrupted due to a system error after the confirmation screen has been shown to the customer | At any point after order confirmation is displayed to the customer | Loss of a confirmed order creates both a customer experience failure and an operational problem for the venue; order durability is a baseline reliability requirement. |
| NFR-005 | [QA-003](#qa-003) | Card Data Isolation | The system must never store, log, or transmit raw card data; all card payment data must be processed exclusively by the integrated third-party payment gateway | During any card payment transaction | GDPR and payment security standards require that raw card data is not handled by the application; full delegation to the payment gateway is the standard compliance approach. |
| NFR-006 | [QA-003](#qa-003) | Admin Session Security | Admin sessions must require authentication and must expire after a configurable period of inactivity [TO BE REFINED] | At all times when the admin interface is active | Unattended authenticated admin sessions present a security risk in a venue environment where physical access to devices may be shared. |
| NFR-007 | [QA-003](#qa-003) | Customer Data Retention | Customer personal data (e.g., name) must not be persisted beyond the completion of the active order, in compliance with GDPR data minimisation principles | For all customer ordering sessions | The venue is based in the EU/EEA; GDPR requires that personal data is retained only for the duration of its stated purpose. Retaining names beyond order completion would require additional legal basis. |
| NFR-008 | [QA-004](#qa-004) | Ordering Flow Completion Without Assistance | A first-time customer must be able to complete the full ordering flow — from QR scan to order submission — without requiring external assistance | For all customers, including those with limited digital literacy, across the full mixed-age user base | The system's core value proposition is to reduce staff involvement in ordering; if customers routinely need help to use the app, the operational efficiency goal is undermined. |
| NFR-009 | [QA-004](#qa-004) | Device Compatibility | The customer-facing app must function correctly on standard mobile phone screen sizes using any modern mobile browser, without requiring installation | For all customers accessing the app via QR code on their personal device | The app is browser-based and must not require any native app installation; compatibility across common iOS and Android browsers is required to serve the full customer base. |
| NFR-010 | [QA-005](#qa-005) | Concurrent Session Capacity | The system must support up to 50 simultaneous active customer sessions without performance degradation | During peak service hours when all tables are occupied | The venue has approximately 10 tables with a combined seating capacity of roughly 40–50 customers; the system must handle full occupancy without degradation. |
| NFR-011 | [QA-006](#qa-006) | Incident Diagnosability | The system must produce sufficient logs and error information to allow the on-standby technical team to diagnose and resolve production incidents | During any production incident or service degradation | A dedicated technical team is on standby; they require adequate observability tooling (logs, error traces) to diagnose and resolve issues promptly without requiring extended downtime. |
| NFR-012 | [QA-006](#qa-006) | Update Deployment | The system must support deployment of updates and patches without requiring extended service interruptions | During scheduled maintenance and patch rollouts | The system must be updatable with minimal downtime to avoid disruption to service hours; zero-downtime or short-window deployment strategies should be supported. |
